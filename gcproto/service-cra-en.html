Authorize a tax representative

From Canada Revenue Agency

How to get started

Overview

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) needs your authorization to deal with another person (such as your spouse or common-law partner, other family member, friend, or accountant) who would act as your representative for income tax matters. You do not need to authorize a representative if another person (such as your spouse or common-law partner, a friend, or your accountant) is only preparing your income tax return.

Eligibility

This service is only for individual tax matters.

  • To authorize a business representative, go to RC59 Business Consent.
  • To authorize a representative for a non-resident, go to Representatives for non-resident accounts
  • To authorize a relay operator, go to Services for Persons with Disabilities.

You do not need to authorize a legal representative with this service. The legal representative must send a complete copy of the legal document giving him or her authority to act in that capacity to the appropriate tax centre.

What you need before you start

You need the RepID if your representative is an individual, the GroupID if your representative is a group, or the Business Number (BN) if your representative is a business.

How long it will take to process your application

Estimated processing times apply only to properly completed applications, and do not include time required for postal delivery. Online requests are processed in five business days and paper applications requests are processed in 20 business days after receipt of the form. Our standard is to meet these targets 90% of the time. Go to service standards for more information.

There are currently no delays in processing applications.

Our current processing time for online applications is 5 business days, and 20 business days for applications submitted by mail.

How to apply

Authorize by mail

Complete Form T1013, Authorizing or Cancelling a Representative and mail it to your tax centre. If you are not sure which tax centre is yours, look on your most recent notice of assessment or notice of reassessment.

If the form is not completed correctly with all the necessary information, the form will not be processed. Also, if we do not receive your completed Form T1013 within 6 months of its signature date, the form will not be processed.

Authorize online

You can authorize a tax representative in My Account.

More information about this service

More information about this service

Managing or cancelling your representatives

If you want a list of your representatives, you can view it in My Account, you can call us at 1-800-959-8281, or you can send your request by mail to your tax centre.

After granting authorization to a new representative, you may want to cancel a representative you had previously granted authorization to. You can immediately cancel an existing authorization by

  • using My Account,
  • by calling us at 1-800-959-8281, or
  • by completing parts 1, 4, and 5 of Form T1013, Authorizing or Cancelling a Representative.

How long will my consent last?

Your consent will stay in effect until:

  • you or your representative cancel it,
  • it reaches the expiry date you choose, or
  • we are notified of your death.

Your representative can immediately delete their own authorization online, make the request by telephone, in person, or in writing.

What can your representative do?

When you authorize a representative, you are letting that person represent you for income tax matters at the level of authorization you specify for the tax year or years you indicate. Income tax matters include issues related to the information on your tax return.

The service that a representative can do on your behalf varies depending on the level of authorization that you have given to him or her.

For security reasons, if your representative contacts or visits us, we will ask him or her for identification. After we confirm your representative's identity, and that he or she is listed as your representative on your account, we will ask for specific information relating to:

  • your notice of assessment or notice of reassessment;
  • your tax return; or
  • other tax documents.

Only you or your legal representative can ask us to change your marital status, address, or direct deposit information. Also, only you or your legal representative will be allowed to authorize, view, and cancel other representatives you have on your file.

Services that representatives can perform

The list of services that representatives can do online on behalf of an individual varies depending on the level of authorization that the individual has consented to. Go to List of services for representatives of individuals for more information.

Levels of authorization

There are two levels of authorization you can give to your representative. By specifying the level of authorization, you are controlling the type of access we give to your representative. If you do not specify a level of authorization, we will assign a level 1 authorization. Go to Levels of authorization for more information.

Legal representative

Only you or your legal representative can ask us to change your marital status, address, or direct deposit information. Also, only you or your legal representative will be allowed to authorize, view and cancel other representatives you have on your file.
